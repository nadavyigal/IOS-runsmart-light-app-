## One-Line Positioning

The AI running coach for beginners and returning runners who need safe daily adaptation — not just faster race plans.

## Full Positioning Statement

RunSmart is a native iOS AI running coach that earns your trust by giving accurate, personalized daily guidance, showing your progress week over week, and never letting you get injured chasing a plan that wasn't built for your body.

## Contrast Line

Not built around Olympian intensity. Built around real-life consistency.

## Target User

- **The Rookie (28–45)**: New or returning runner, runs 1–3x/week, no wearable or basic Apple Watch. Tried NRC or a free training plan, got injured or lost motivation by week 3. Needs a plan that feels calibrated to their fitness level, visible proof of improvement, and confidence they won't hurt themselves.
- **The Striver (25–40) with a wearable**: Intermediate runner with Garmin or Apple Watch, generates data they don't fully trust. Churned from Runna because the plan didn't adapt when life got in the way. Needs an additional intelligence layer on their wearable data, adaptive coaching with a "why".

## Acquisition Wedge

Runners who have churned from Runna, specifically citing injury or plan rigidity.

## Jobs To Be Done

1. *When I wake up*, I want to know if I should run and at what effort — in plain language, not numbers — so I can make a confident decision in 30 seconds.
2. *When my plan says something that feels wrong for my body today*, I want RunSmart to explain why it's right (or adapt it if it isn't), so I don't have to choose between the plan and my instincts.
3. *When I finish a workout*, I want to see that it contributed to something — a streak, a fitness milestone, a coach reaction — so I feel the progress building.
4. *When I'm tired or sore*, I want RunSmart to catch it before I do and hold me back with a reason I believe, so I trust it more than I trust my impulse to push through.

## Retention Thesis

Users stay past week 2 when three things are true simultaneously:
1. **Personalization accuracy** — the plan and daily recommendation feel calibrated to *my* body and fitness, not a template
2. **Visible progress** — I can see and feel that I'm improving, even if small
3. **Safety confidence** — RunSmart has my back; when it holds me back, it tells me why in coach language, and I trust it

These three together = earned trust. Earned trust = retention. Retention = word-of-mouth growth.

## Three Things to Remember

1. **A plan isn't a coach.** Runna delivers a schedule. RunSmart delivers a coach that reads your body every morning.
2. **Safety is a feature.** The 10% rule, readiness gate, and conservative defaults aren't fine print — they're the product.
3. **Personal growth, not race times.** Runna is organized around finish lines. RunSmart is organized around you.

## Before / After

Before: static PDF plans, generic apps, expensive coaches, second-guessing every missed session.

After: a plan that flexes when life changes, a clear next session, a debrief that says "this run was as expected" or "back off", and confidence that I'm on track for my goal.

## Category

AI running coach. Adjacent: training plan apps, coach-on-demand, run analysis tools.

## Differentiators

- Adaptive plan generation that responds to actual sessions (not a fixed weekly grid)
- Post-run debrief in plain language
- Readiness check tied to recovery (safety-first defaults)
- Race strategy at the right moments
- Integrations with Garmin / Apple Health that turn data into decisions, not dashboards
- Conversational coaching — explains the "why" behind every recommendation

## Anti-Positioning

- Not a generic AI chatbot wrapper
- Not a step-tracker dashboard
- Not a static plan PDF
- Not a marketplace for human coaches
- Not built for elites or sub-elites
- Do not position as "AI-powered" as the primary hero claim — it is true but generic in a now-crowded field

## Battlecard Rules (do not claim)

- Do not claim "Runna lacks Garmin/COROS depth" — they support structured workout sync to Garmin and COROS.
- Do not claim "Runna is unsafe" — softer: "some users and press report overload; Runna has responded with beginner plan updates."
- Do not claim "Runna has no AI" — they have AI-powered features; RunSmart's edge is conversational + readiness-first + daily adaptation.
- Do not use generic "AI running coach" positioning alone — the space now includes Kotcha, KASI, URUNN, Strava Athlete Intelligence, Garmin Connect+, Apple Workout Buddy.
- Do not claim outcome guarantees ("faster", "fewer injuries") without cited internal data.
- Do not claim Garmin / Apple integration depth beyond what is actually live in the current build.

## Voice And Tone

- Direct, honest, encouraging without being sappy
- Treats the user as a capable adult
- Says when a session was hard, not just "good job"
- Avoids hype ("revolutionary", "AI-powered" as a hero claim)
- Safety explanations framed as "here's what the data says" not "you can't do this"

## Bound Claims (must be backed)

- Adaptive plan logic — must reflect actual product behavior
- Garmin / Apple integration depth — only claim what is implemented
- Outcome claims (faster, fitter, fewer injuries) — only with cited internal data or external study
